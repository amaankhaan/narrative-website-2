Earth

OUR HOME PLANET

Earth 4.5 billion years old, our home planet, is the third celestial body within our solar system, after Venus. It’s the only habitual planet we know of so far, inhabited by living organisms. With a rocky structure and vast amounts liquid water (70% of the surface) which surround islands composed of minerals, stones and rock. Combined with the ideal atmosphere made up of nitrogen and oxygen, It provides the ideal temperature to make life abundant here. Earths structure is made up of four main layers, the inner core at the centre is composed of iron and nickel and reaches temperatures high as 5,400c, enveloped by the outer core composed of thick iron and Nickel fluids expands out to 1400 miles finishing with a ocean like chamber of hot molten rock- the mantle. And the crust (the surface we live on)

THE MOON

Formed during the construction of planet earth., the moon is also known as a natural satellite- as it’s the largest orbiting item around the earth, reflecting light during the night, it’s the fifth largest natural satellite in the solar system. The gravitational pull generates total forces and forces the ocean to move on the moon’s axis.
