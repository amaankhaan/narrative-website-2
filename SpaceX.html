SPACE X

Elon Musk

"Elon Musk is the CEO and co-founder of SpaceX, Tesla, Neuralink and The Boring Company. As lead designer at SpaceX, Elon oversees the development of rockets and spacecraft for missions to Earth orbit and ultimately to other planets. The SpaceX Falcon 1 was the first privately developed liquid fuel rocket to reach orbit, in 2008.

In 2017, SpaceX made further history by re-flying both a Falcon 9 rocket and Dragon spacecraft for the first time. By pioneering the development of reusable rockets, SpaceX is pursuing the long-term goal of making humans a multi-planet species." The royal society, No date:Online

What is Space X currently doing?

In the present time, along with leading world class astrophysics, engineering research. Space X is committed to improving the life of the everyday user. The multinational company is designing and building safe, reliable and demisable satellites for their high reliability along with investigating the second gen’ Moon astronauts which will be the first to fly in the Artemis 4 mission (Space X x NASA) in 2027.

Space X future goal?

We all dream of what it would be like on different planets, by 2023 Space X promises to inhabit and send the first of the human civilisation to set foot on mars, via starship. In the future, Space X plans its missions to send small fleets (also known as Starship mini!) funded completely by public/private partnerships which brings the hope of built infrastructure and colonization the red planet.

Reusable Rockets?

Falcon 9 is a reusable, two-stage rocket designed and manufactured by SpaceX for the reliable and safe transport of people and payloads into Earth orbit and beyond. Falcon 9 is the world’s first orbital class reusable rocket. Reusability allows SpaceX to refly the most expensive parts of the rocket, which in turn drives down the cost of space access.
